# QARA Engagement Guide
# Sword Health ISC — Procurement Knowledge Base
# Sword Insighter | Last updated: 2026-04-18

When and how to involve Quality Assurance and Regulatory Affairs (QARA) in procurement decisions.

---

## Primary contact

**Sofia Lourenço** — Expert QSE
- Channel: Slack DM + email (always in Portuguese)
- Role: Supplier Quality, SQA process, Qualio, FDA cross-references, regulatory questions
- Do NOT contact João Quirino or Bianca Lourenço directly. Sofia is the first point of contact.

**Bianca Lourenço** — Senior Manager, Regulatory Affairs
- Escalation only. Sofia will loop Bianca when needed.
- Handles complex regulatory decisions, FDA submissions, UDI strategy.

**André's role:** relay. André coordinates between suppliers and QARA. He does NOT decide on regulatory or quality matters. He does NOT approve LoE content. He does NOT negotiate SQA terms.

---

## When to loop Sofia

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Supplier selected (Shortlisted) for Pulse | Initiate SQA process. Send supplier docs to Sofia. |
| Supplier provides LoE (Letter of Evaluation) | Forward to Sofia for review. |
| Supplier raises ISO 13485 or CE certification question | Relay question to Sofia. |
| NDA includes QARA-relevant IP clauses | Flag to Sofia (and Bradley) before signing. |
| Supplier asks about FDA registration process | Relay to Sofia. Do NOT answer directly. |
| UDI / DI questions from supplier | Relay to Sofia. Do NOT interpret. |
| New regulatory change that may affect Pulse specs | Sofia flags these proactively; confirm with her before acting. |

---

## What André collects from supplier for QARA

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| ISO 13485 certificate (current) | Confirms quality system |
| CE marking documentation | Confirms EU market compliance |
| FDA registration / 510(k) reference | Confirms US market compliance |
| Quality manual (or summary) | SQA review input |
| LoE (Letter of Evaluation) | Supplier's regulatory self-assessment |
| Clinical accuracy validation data | Pulse: required for BP/scale devices |

André requests these from the supplier. Sofia reviews. André does not assess them.

---

## SQA process outline (Pulse)

1. Supplier is Shortlisted → André notifies Sofia in Portuguese: "Sofia, [Supplier] foi selecionado para o Pulse. Posso partilhar os documentos para iniciar o SQA?"
2. Sofia confirms and starts Qualio record.
3. André collects documents from supplier and forwards to Sofia (Google Drive or Qualio upload link).
4. Sofia reviews. May request additional documents or corrections.
5. If audit needed (on-site or desk-based): Sofia coordinates. André supports logistics.
6. Sofia prepares SQA draft → Bianca reviews if needed → André receives final version.
7. André sends SQA to supplier for signature (usually attached to first PO terms or as standalone agreement).
8. Signed SQA filed in Qualio. André logs milestone in Notion Outreach section.

**Timeline:** 4–8 weeks. Depends on Sofia's queue and supplier's document readiness.

---

## QTA (Quality Technical Agreement)

Different from SQA. QTA is more technical and product-specific.

- Not always required at initial onboarding — depends on product type and risk level.
- Sofia determines whether a QTA is needed.
- If required: same relay pattern as SQA.

---

## UDI / DI (Pulse-specific)

UDI = Unique Device Identifier. Required for FDA registration of Pulse devices.

- UDI-DI ownership is being aligned with Transtek (OI in Open Items). See Pulse context.
- André's role: relay supplier questions, coordinate with Mika (Transtek RA contact) and Sofia.
- Who decides: Bianca (regulatory strategy). Sofia (implementation). André (logistics of getting docs).
- Do NOT commit to a UDI-DI assignment without Sofia and Bianca alignment.

---

## Communication format for QARA requests

Always in Portuguese. Example:

> "Sofia, podes dar uma vista de olhos ao certificado ISO 13485 do [Supplier]? Estou a avaliar se podemos avançar para SQA. Envio-te o documento por aqui ou prefereres Drive?"

Short, direct, no technical decisions embedded in the message. Just relay.

---

## What NOT to do

- Do NOT interpret regulatory requirements for the supplier. Forward the question to Sofia.
- Do NOT agree to regulatory timelines on behalf of QARA. Sofia owns that calendar.
- Do NOT share supplier LoEs or quality documents externally without Sofia's review.
- Do NOT loop Bianca without Sofia's recommendation (except for emergencies escalated by the supplier or legal).
